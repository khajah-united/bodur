What is the Beginning? And How Was the End?

The beginning, at times, was without documentation, repeating absence, repeating silence. Even when documentation existed, it did not do justice to the story, the details, those who were there, those who lived the experience, those who created the event, those who were the event, those who were everything. Documentation did not honor them, memory did not honor them, forgetting did not honor them, absence did not honor them, the end did not honor them, no one did.

Even the honest narrators of that adventure and its lingering details belonged to a unique kind that differed and passed. The story remained incomplete, the beginning and the end remained, and so did the details.

This story is more than letters, more than documents, more than names, more than titles.

What is Heritage? Definition and Importance:

Heritage, in its simplest sense, is what past generations left behind for the present ones. It is the legacy of ancestors, serving as lessons from the past and guidance for the future. In civilization, heritage is like the roots of a tree; the deeper and more branched the roots, the stronger and more resilient the tree becomes against the challenges of time. Scientifically, heritage is a cultural field of study focusing on traditional or folk culture, highlighting it from historical, geographical, social, and psychological perspectives. Folk heritage includes people’s customs and traditions.

It also reflects opinions, ideas, and emotions passed down through generations. The largest part of folk heritage consists of folk tales such as poems, sung verses, stories of mythical creatures, heroic tales, and legends. It also includes arts, crafts, dances, games, entertainment, children’s songs and stories, proverbs, riddles, puzzles, superstitions, celebrations, and religious festivals.
